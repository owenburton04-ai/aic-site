The mission

We figure out how AI is really used across finance.

Applied Intelligence Collective is an independent, student-founded research community studying how AI is actually being used across finance: what genuinely works, what's overhyped, and the real workflows behind it. You research a question you care about, publish real work, and build fluency that carries into your career.

Honest about what we are.

Independent and new. We're not affiliated with any university, employer, or fund, and the program is unpaid. What you get is real research experience, a portfolio, and a network. The full detail lives in the program overview.

Your question, your call

Four areas. You scope your own question.

There's no fixed topic menu. You pick a two-month question inside one of four finance areas, and your team lead helps you shape it in week 1.

Your two months

Eight weeks, start to showcase. Fall dates TBD

  2. 02

  3. 03

    Weeks 7–8

    Synthesize & Present

    Final paper and deck, delivered at the Research Showcase.

A mid-point checkpoint keeps momentum.

Around week 4 the whole cohort shares preliminary findings. It's the single best way to keep a remote group from fizzling in the back half. The weekly rhythm, research methods, and team structure are all laid out in the program overview.

An opportunity, not a requirement

Some teams will do real client work.

AIC is beginning to offer research to real companies: a team of researchers on a company's own question about how AI is used in their industry. Strong researchers can be staffed on these paid engagements, alongside their own research. It's optional, and it's real experience with a real deliverable and a real deadline.
