The founders

Two friends who wanted the real story.

Owen Burton and Jackson Delgrosso are best friends headed into finance. Over the past year they got hooked on the same two things: the investing, funding, and structuring side of finance, and how fast AI is reshaping all of it. They set out to understand where the two actually meet.

Why we started

As students, we ran into two problems everyone entering finance faces: finding real-world experience outside the classroom, and understanding how AI is going to change the career ahead of us.

What finally made it click was hands-on work: an internship where our two interests came together and we learned by doing. The more we learned, the more we wanted to give other students the same shot. So we built Applied Intelligence Collective: a place with the structure, environment, and accountability to research how AI is really being used across finance.

What we believe

  1. 01

    Learn by doing

    The classroom only takes you so far. Real understanding comes from digging into the work yourself, which is exactly what a member's two months here look like.

  2. 02

    A shared vision compounds

    When students come together with a shared vision, our learning and experience compound, and that's what prepares us for the careers ahead.

  3. 03

    Knowledge before action

    Great action always comes from great knowledge. That's why we start with research: honest, sourced, and first-hand, never recycled hype.

Where we are now

A founding cohort, already at work.

What started as a shared frustration is now a working research community. Here's where things stand.

  1. 01

    A founding cohort

    Selected from 30+ applicants (with early encouragement from professors, finance clubs, and industry professionals) and organized into research groups across finance.

  2. 02

    Research, published in the open

    Weekly briefs and final papers publish right here on the site, each leading with the honest call, sourced and credited.

  3. 03

    Just the beginning

    We're building this in public and adapting as we go, with more cohorts, more research, and more of finance's real story to tell.
